Calkiní Campeche

History

Calkiní comes from the Mayan language and is made up of the words: cal «throat», and kin «sun» and the suffix «I», like the preposition «of» which means «sun's throat». It is estimated that the settlement of the Canul lineage was located in a place called U tuuc caan (a corner of heaven), in the current city of Calkiní. This happened between 1441 and 1443, after the destruction of Mayapán. After this event, the Canul founded nearby towns. From then on, Franciscan temples and convents were opened. Buildings were erected and Christian images were carved on their walls. Despite this, the inhabitants did not lose their Mayan customs and beliefs, whose language is still preserved in a good part of the municipality. On February 14, 1824, the town of Calkiní was declared a village. And in 1858, he adhered to the geography of the State of Campeche. Calkiní received its city title on November 30, 1918. Later, the surface was covered with schools and parks; spaces for sports and recreation were opened.

Food

There are many typical foods of the area such as "Frijol con puerco", "Pan de cazon", the typical "panucho", to name a few, but my favorite is seafood meal, since Calkini is near a fishing area there are many dishes that involve it. One in particular is a type of fish called "Rubia" which is served fried and is delicious.

About the city

Calkíni (from Yucatec Maya: Throat of the Sun) is a city in the Mexican state of Campeche, located north of the territory near the border with the State of Yucatán, in the region known as "Camino Real". It is the head of the municipality of Calkiní.

Points of interest

There are a few places that you might want to visit:
  1. Mayan arch: The structure, based on an architecture outlined in the false Mayan arch, consists of two small arches to protect pedestrians on their way to any of the directions of the road, and two monumental arches for two separate vehicular lanes. It has two towers, with 72 iron steps each, and which functioned as a snail to go up to the viewpoint, from which a wide panorama of the town and some peripheral areas could be observed.
  2. House of culture: In it, painting workshops, folkloric dance, music, Mayan language, among others, are taught. It has a talented group of children that make up the Municipal Symphony Orchestra.
  3. San luis obispo church: It was declared a National Monument on April 4, 1952. It is a building with a single nave, with a three-section bell tower built in 1774. It is one of the most beautiful churches in the Mexican Southeast.
  4. Centro ecoturístico "El remate": The "El Remate" Ecotourism Center is an incredible place to enjoy a day of relaxation and fun in contact with nature. It is a crystal clear water hole where you can swim and do different activities. This spring is connected to the sea through an eight kilometer artificial channel made many years ago to facilitate trade in this area with other places. In its surroundings you will see a large number of palm trees. "El remate" has a depth that varies between 1.5 meters and 4 meters, which makes it ideal for the whole family. Here you can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, kayaking or zip lining surrounded by a beautiful landscape.
  5. "Isla arena": It is located in the municipality of Calkiní, in the Petenes-Ría Celestún Natural Park, it is a fishing port; In your stay you will find lodging and restaurant services, where you can taste a rich gastronomy.
  6. Museum to Pedro Infante:Museum dedicated to honoring the greatest legend of Mexican cinema, Pedro Infante. It exhibits a collection of personal items and the original wardrobe from his most representative films. It offers the public conferences, workshops, artistic interventions, mini-concerts, micro-exhibitions.
